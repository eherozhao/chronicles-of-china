## The Coup at Chenqiao and Founding

In 960, the military commander Zhao Kuangyin was acclaimed emperor by his troops at Chenqiao — his generals draping a yellow robe over his shoulders. As Emperor Taizu, he drew a sharp lesson from the Tang's collapse: never again allow regional warlords to accumulate unchecked power. In the celebrated "Cup of Wine" episode, he persuaded his senior generals to exchange their commands for titles and estates. The resulting dynasty was deliberately civilian in character, elevating scholar-officials above military men — a policy that defined the Song for its entire 319-year lifespan.

## Economic Prosperity

The Northern Song was arguably the most commercially sophisticated society in the premodern world. Fixed market quarters gave way to open street commerce; shops and food stalls operated around the clock. Popular entertainment — storytelling, shadow puppetry, variety theatre — flourished in urban pleasure quarters. The capital Bianjing (modern Kaifeng), depicted in Zhang Zeduan's masterpiece *Along the River During the Qingming Festival*, teemed with canal traffic, restaurants, and workshops. Economic historians estimate Song China produced 25–30% of global GDP.

## The Age of Four Great Inventions

The Song Dynasty saw the practical maturation of China's four great inventions. Bi Sheng invented movable type printing around 1040, enabling books to circulate at unprecedented scale. Gunpowder weapons — fire arrows, bombs, and early firearms — transformed siege warfare. The magnetic compass was applied to navigation, expanding China's maritime reach across Southeast Asia and into the Indian Ocean. Paper money (*jiaozi*), the world's first banknotes, emerged in Sichuan — a product of both a vibrant commercial economy and refined papermaking.

## Key Figures

- **Emperor Taizu (Zhao Kuangyin)** — founder; demilitarised the state and established the primacy of civil governance
- **Wang Anshi** — reforming chancellor whose "New Policies" introduced state loans to farmers and local militia; his reforms ignited a generation of factional conflict with the conservatives led by Sima Guang
- **Su Shi (Su Dongpo)** — poet, calligrapher, gastronome, and official; his verse "The great river flows east" inaugurated the bold *ci* lyric tradition
- **Shen Kuo** — polymath whose *Dream Pool Essays* recorded movable type, magnetic declination, petroleum, and dozens of other scientific observations
- **Fan Zhongyan** — statesman and essayist; his line "Be first to bear the world's hardships, last to enjoy its pleasures" became a creed for generations of Chinese officials

## Cultural Achievements

Song culture was both refined and intellectually restless. Neo-Confucianism — synthesised by the Cheng brothers and later Zhu Xi — reimagined the classical tradition for a new era. The *ci* lyric form reached its heights in the hands of Liu Yong, Su Shi, and Li Qingzhao, the great female poet of the Southern Song. Ceramic art peaked at the imperial kilns: the five great kilns (Ru, Guan, Ge, Jun, Ding) produced wares prized above all others for their understated glazes.

## The Jingkang Catastrophe

In 1125, the Jurchen Jin Dynasty — former allies against the Liao — turned their armies south. In 1127, Bianjing fell. Emperors Huizong and Qinzong, along with thousands of courtiers and palace women, were marched north into captivity. The *Jingkang Humiliation* (靖康之耻) ended the Northern Song. A remnant court fled south, eventually establishing a new capital at Lin'an (present-day Hangzhou) — the beginning of the Southern Song.
